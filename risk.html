INSIGHTS

Risk Management

Risk management is integrated into every investment decision. Our goal is to protect against permanent capital impairment while maintaining the flexibility to act during dislocations.
Risk is evaluated continuously—before, during, and after an investment.
Our Framework

We view risk as the probability and magnitude of permanent loss, not short-term volatility. Our process emphasizes identifying fragile business models, avoiding asymmetric downside, and ensuring portfolio liquidity under stress.

Risk lenses applied to every position
BUSINESS RISK

Durability of the Franchise

Competitive advantages, pricing power, and sensitivity to demand or regulation.

FINANCIAL RISK

Balance Sheet & Leverage

Debt structure, refinancing needs, and the ability to self-fund through cycles.

VALUATION RISK

Margin of Safety

We invest when return potential is attractive across a range of outcomes, not only in best cases.

LIQUIDITY RISK

Ability to Act Under Stress

Position sizing considers market depth, spreads, and correlation in dislocations.

Portfolio controls
  • Concentration discipline: A small number of positions is acceptable when supported by deep research and strict sizing.
  • Liquidity awareness: We consider exit feasibility and avoid structures that force selling at the wrong time.
  • Scenario testing: We stress-test earnings, margins, funding, and demand shocks across macro regimes.
  • Ongoing monitoring: We track thesis drift, governance signals, and balance-sheet changes continuously.
  • Decision documentation: Key assumptions are recorded and reviewed to reduce behavioral bias.
How we respond when risk increases

When conditions deteriorate or new information changes the risk profile, we reassess the investment case, update scenarios, and adjust sizing. If downside becomes asymmetric or thesis integrity erodes, we act decisively.

RISK CULTURE

Discipline over prediction.

We do not attempt to forecast every market move. Instead, we build portfolios that can endure uncertainty and remain liquid enough to take advantage of opportunity.

  • • Prefer resilient cash generation
  • • Avoid fragile financing structures
  • • Size positions for downside
  • • Stress-test before conviction
  • • Reassess when facts change
