SUSTAINABILITY

Sustainability Overview

Wentworth Capital integrates material environmental, social, and governance considerations into research and stewardship where relevant. Our objective is practical: strengthen risk awareness, improve decision quality, and support long-term value creation.
We focus on materiality, evidence, and accountability.
Our approach

We apply a materiality-based lens. Sustainability considerations are evaluated when they are relevant to cash flows, governance, regulatory exposure, reputational risk, or long-term franchise durability. We do not treat ESG as a standalone label; we treat it as part of disciplined fundamental analysis.

Core principles
MATERIALITY

Focus on what changes outcomes

We prioritize factors that can meaningfully affect long-term returns and downside risk.

EVIDENCE

Prefer measurable indicators

We rely on data, disclosures, and repeatable assessment—avoiding broad narratives.

STEWARDSHIP

Engage where it matters

We support governance and transparency improvements when they are value-relevant.

ACCOUNTABILITY

Document decisions

Key assumptions and risks are recorded and revisited as facts evolve.

How sustainability is integrated
  • Research: Assess material ESG factors alongside industry structure, unit economics, and governance quality.
  • Valuation: Reflect long-term risks (regulatory, transition, operational) in scenario ranges and margin of safety.
  • Risk monitoring: Track signals that may indicate rising operational, social, or governance risk.
  • Stewardship: Engage selectively on disclosure, board practices, capital allocation, and risk oversight.
  • Reporting: Communicate our process and the key drivers we consider, with appropriate limitations.
Governance and oversight

Sustainability is overseen within our broader governance and risk framework. Accountability is defined across investment, risk, and operational functions. Where relevant, we incorporate sustainability considerations into investment committee discussions and ongoing monitoring.

WHAT WE PRIORITIZE

Practical, decision-relevant work.

Our focus is not on broad statements. We prioritize governance quality, transparency, and risk controls that protect long-term owners.

  • • Governance: board oversight, incentives, capital allocation
  • • Disclosure: clarity, consistency, auditability where possible
  • • Risk: operational resilience, regulatory exposure, reputation
  • • Stakeholders: integrity and responsible practices
